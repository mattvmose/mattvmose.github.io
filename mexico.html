About the Circuit

Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez is one of the most atmospheric races on the F1 calendar. Located in Mexico City, the circuit sits at over 2,200 meters (7,200 feet) above sea level, making it the highest altitude track on the calendar. The thin air of the track affects downforce, cooling, and engine performance, creating unique challenges for teams and drivers. The circuit features a mix of high-speed straights and technical corners, with the iconic "Peraltada" corner being one of the most famous in F1 history.

Track Facts

  • Location: Mexico City, Mexico
  • First F1 Race: 1963
  • Length: 4.304 km (2.674 miles)
  • Number of Turns: 17
  • Lap Record: 1:17.774 by Valtteri Bottas (2021)
  • Signature Feature: The Foro Sol stadium section
  • The altitude reduces air density by ~25%, dramatically affecting car performance
  • The track was named after the Rodríguez brothers, who were prominent figures in Mexican motorsports
  • The peraltada corner is one of the most challenging and famous sections of the circuit

Why Fans Love Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez

The Mexican Grand Prix is renowned for its electric crowd, with fans packing the stadium section to cheer on their favorite drivers. The unique altitude challenges create unpredictable racing conditions, while the vibrant culture of Mexico City adds to the overall experience.
