About the Circuit

The Circuit of the Americas (COTA) is one of the most iconic modern F1 tracks. Opened in 2012, it features dramatic elevation changes, a steep climb into turn 1, and a fast, flowing layout inspired by Silverstone's famous Maggots and Becketts chicane.

Track Facts

  • Location: Austin, Texas
  • First F1 Race: 2012
  • Length: 5.513 km (3.425 miles)
  • Number of Turns: 20
  • Lap Record: 1:32.029 by Max Verstappen (2021)
  • Signature Feature: 133-foot hill into Turn 1
  • There are approximately 240,000 honeybees kept at the circuit, and honey is sold to fans regularly

Why Fans Love COTA

COTA blends American entertainment with world-class racing. The event includes concerts, fan zones, and some of the largest crowds on the F1 calendar. The track layout encourages overtaking and produces unpredictable racing scenarios, making it a favorite among drivers and fans alike.
