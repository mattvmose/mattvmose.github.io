About the Circuit

The Miami International Autodrome is a temporary circuit built around the Hard ROck stadium in Miami Gardens. Debuting in 2022, the track blends fast-flowing sections with tight technical corners, creating a layout that challenges drivers while offering fans a festival-like atmosphere. The circuit was designed to mimic the feel of a permanent track despite being constructed on temporary infrastructure each year.

Track Facts

  • Location: Miami Gardens, Florida
  • First F1 Race: 2022
  • Length: 5.412 km (3.363 miles)
  • Number of Turns: 19
  • Lap Record: 1:29.708 by Max Verstappen (2023)
  • Signature Feature: Marina-themed fan zone and the long back straight into Turn 17
  • The "fake marina" became one of the most talked about features of the inaugural event.

Why Fans Love the Miami International Autodrome

The Miami Grand Prix blends Formula 1 with Miami's signature high energy culture, celebrity appearances, vibrant fan zones, and a festival style environment, along with being only 20 miles north of downtown Miami, with its vibrant nightclubs and party scene. The circuit's long straight and heavy breaking zones encourage overtaking, while the stadium setting gives fans excellent viewing angles. Its combination of entertainment and competitive racing has quickyl made it one of the most popular stops on the F1 calendar.
