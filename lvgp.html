About the Circuit

The Las Vegas Strip Circuit debuted in 2023 and instatntly became one of the most iconic night races in Formula 1. The track runs directly down the Las Vegas Strip, surrouned by neon lights, resorts, casinos, and massive crowds. Designed for high speeds, the circuit features long straights and heavy breaking zones that challenge drivers and create dramatic racing moments.

Track Facts

  • Location: Las Vegas, Nevada
  • First F1 Race: 2023
  • Length: 6.201 km (3.853 miles)
  • Number of Turns: 17
  • Lap Record: 1:33.365 Max Verstappen (2025)
  • Signature Feature: 2 km flat-out blast down the Las Vegas Strip
  • The race starts at 10:00 p.m. local time to maximize the nighttime spectacle

Why Fans Love the Las Vegas Strip Circuit

The Las Vegas Grand Prix is pure entertainment, with a high-speed night race surrounded by the energy of the Strip. Fans enjoy concerts, celebrity appearances, and a festive atmosphere unlike any other race on the calendar. The long straights and tight corners create unpredictable racing and frequent skirmishes between drivers.
