About the Circuit

Circuit Gilles Villeneuve is one of Formula 1's most historic and beloved tracks. Located on Notre Dame Island in Montréal, the circuit blends long straights with tight chicanes, rewarding precision and punishing mistakes. The track is known for its scenic setting and passionate Canadian fans. The track is named after former F1 driver Gilles Villeneuve, who tragically lost his life in a crash at the Belgian Grand Prix in 1982.

Track Facts

  • Location: Montréal, Quebec, Canada
  • First F1 Race: 1978
  • Length: 4.361 km (2.710 miles)
  • Number of Turns: 14
  • Lap Record: 1:13.078 by Valtteri Bottas (2019)
  • Signature Feature: The Wall of Champions at the exit of the final chicane
  • The circuit sits on a man-made island built for Expo 67

Why Fans Love Circuit Gilles Villeneuve

The Canadian Grand Prix is known for its passionate fans, unpredictable weather, and thrilling racing. The combination of long straights and heavy breaking zones encourages overtaking, while the Montréal setting offers world-class food, culture, and nightlife for visiting fans.
